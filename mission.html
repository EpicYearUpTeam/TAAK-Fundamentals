About Year Up Chicago

Year Up Chicago is a year-long workforce program that serves young adults from ages 18-24 by providing training for students in both information technology and cooperate culture. Some of the topics covered are summarized below.



Hardware
"Intro to A+ focuses on learning the basic concepts circulating throughout the industry. Students get the chance to work with these different technologies in the hardware lab. We want our students to train their minds to think critically and outside the box, as technology is a dynamically complex, yet enjoyable field to embark on. Students learn about: the motherboard, CPU, RAM, ROM, the BIOS, troubleshooting, basic networking, crimping LAN cables, preventative maintenance, printer maintenance, virtualization, and last but not least they learn to love technology. We challenge them to get in touch with their inner nerd."

Instructor Ashley Russell




"Intro to Applications introduces students to basic computer concepts. The course also introduces students to some of the more practical features of Office 2010 and 2013. Students experience hands-on instruction in word processing, spreadsheets, databases, and professional presentations. This course prepares students to succeed in both college and the business world by enabling them to write reports, analyze and chart data, and prepare presentations."

Instructor Ivana Tate
Software




Web Development
"Intro to Web Development is a beginner-level course that intends to introduce students to front end web development. Focusing on HTML5 and CSS3, Intro to Web Development offers the opportunity to learn the basics of creating attractive and functional websites. Throughout the entire L&D phase, students will develop their skills in order to create a capstone project that requires them to create and publish a website. The hope of this course is to spark an interest in learning about web development and to encourage the students to continue learning beyond what the course covers."

Instructor Lee Roberts




"Business Communications focuses on written and verbal communication within the business world. Students learn the mechanics of business e-mails, resumes, cover letters, and LinkedIn profiles and gain significant experience in public speaking through individual speeches, group presentations, networking, and interviews. Students also practice effective work techniques including note taking, summarizing, and time management and learn and implement the basics of project management through an end of semester educational website design project."

Instructor Elizabeth Pearson
